GEMSBOK

The gemsbok or gemsbuck (Oryx gazella) is a large antelope in the Oryx genus. It is native to the arid regions of Southern Africa, such as the Kalahari Desert. The gemsbok is depicted on the coat of arms of Namibia, where the current population of the species is estimated at 373,000 individuals.

Gemsbok are light brownish-grey to tan in color, with lighter patches toward the bottom rear of the rump. Their tails are long and black in color. A blackish stripe extends from the chin down the lower edge of the neck, through the juncture of the shoulder and leg along the lower flank of each side to the blackish section of the rear leg.

They are mainly desert-dwelling and do not depend on drinking water to supply their physiological needs. They can reach running speeds of up to 60 km/h (37 mph).

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM?

You will find the gemsboks just beyond Panda Canyon.

Did You Know?

  • Gemsbok can survive without water for extended periods
  • Both male and female gemsbok have long, straight horns
  • Their horns can grow up to 85 cm (33 in) long
