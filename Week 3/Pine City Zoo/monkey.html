DUSKY LEAF MONKEY

The dusky leaf monkey, spectacled langur, or spectacled leaf monkey (Trachypithecus obscurus) is a species of primate in the family Cercopithecidae. These monkeys are known for their distinctive appearance with white rings around their eyes that resemble spectacles.

Dusky leaf monkeys are primarily leaf-eaters, though they also consume fruits, flowers, and occasionally insects. They live in groups of 5-20 individuals with a complex social structure. These monkeys are highly arboreal, spending most of their time in the trees where they move gracefully through the canopy.

Their digestive systems are specially adapted to break down tough leaves, with a complex stomach similar to that of cows. They are generally quiet animals but communicate through various facial expressions and body postures.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM?

You will find the monkeys at the monkey cage near the Monkey Trail.

Did You Know?

  • Newborn dusky leaf monkeys are bright orange, changing to gray as they mature
  • They have special bacteria in their stomachs to help digest leaves
  • These monkeys can leap up to 8 meters between trees
