INSECT HOUSE

Step into the fascinating world of insects at our award-winning Insect House, home to one of the largest and most diverse insect collections in the world. Discover creatures from every continent in carefully recreated natural habitats.

Featured Exhibits

Butterfly Pavilion

Walk among hundreds of free-flying butterflies in our tropical greenhouse. Watch as they feed, fly, and sometimes even land on visitors!

Giant Insect Display

See some of the world's largest insects including Goliath beetles, giant walking sticks, and enormous tarantulas.

Honey Bee Observation Hive

Watch live honey bees at work in our glass-enclosed hive. Learn about their complex social structure and vital role in pollination.

Special Events

  • Bug Chef Demonstrations: Saturdays at 11am - See how insects are used in cuisine around the world
  • Meet the Keeper: Daily at 2pm - Learn about insect care from our experts
  • Night Crawlers Tour: First Friday of each month - Special evening viewing of nocturnal insects

Did You Know?

Insects make up about 80% of all animal species on Earth! Our exhibit showcases over 200 species, each with unique adaptations for survival.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND IT?

The Insect House is at the eastern side of the zoo, next to the Monkey Trail. Look for the giant anthill sculpture at the entrance.

Visitor Tips

  • The Butterfly Pavilion is warm and humid - dress accordingly
  • Flash photography is not allowed as it disturbs the insects
  • Ask about our "Junior Entomologist" program for kids
  • Visit in the morning when many insects are most active
